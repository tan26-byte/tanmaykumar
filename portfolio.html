About Me

  • I am a 3 rd Electrical & Computer Engineering student at MIT-WPU, Pune.

  • My primary interest lies in understanding how physical systems behave and how they can be measured, controlled, and improved through thoughtful engineering design.

  • I am particularly drawn to analog circuit design, instrumentation, embedded hardware systems, and computer science

  • I prefer building systems from fundamentals, working with discrete components, signal conditioning stages, and low-level interfaces, rather than relying solely on pre-built abstractions.

  • I find value in debugging hardware at the low level layers, and focusing on the real-life implications of my system design and architecture.

  • Through my projects, I aim to combine theoretical concepts from coursework with practical implementation.

  • Whether designing a tachometer using comparators and counters, or a smart-home automation system using the versatile ESP8266, I focus on clarity, robustness, and understanding.

  • I believe strong fundamentals and careful system-level thinking form the foundation of good engineering.
